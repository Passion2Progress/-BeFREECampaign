THE CAUSE

Over the last three years, FREE have supplied clean water to refugee camps, provided guardians for children that have lost their parents to the insurgency, delivered  medical  supplies  to  health  clinics,  and offered  adult  women  literacy  and  vocational programs. FREE’s Empowerment Program helps women gain entrepreneurship skills with  which  they can  rebuild  their  lives  and  eventually  become  independent.



Water Crisis

​More than 75% of water and sanitation infrastructure has been destroyed​ ​or damaged since the beginning of the conflict, leaving 3.6 million people without even basic water services.

Nearly 80 percent of the IDPs state that their ​access to water is poor​.

​ Over 32,000 IDPs in Borno State last year ​suffered from Cholera​, a disease caused by ingestion of contaminated food and water.


Displacement

Over 1.62 million people have fled their homes in Northeast Nigeria as a result of the violence.

The total number of internally displaced people (IDPs) in Nigeria at large totals over 2 million.

Nearly half a million homes and 700 public buildings have been destroyed by the conflict, along with 1,200 schools, nearly 800 health facilities, and 1,600 water supply sources. More than half of all children in the north-east are out of school.

Boko Haram Violence

Over ​15,000 Nigerians have lost their lives​​ to Boko Haram’s terror.

In April 2014, attackers raided Chibok deep in northeastern Nigeria and kidnapped 276 school girls. Most of whom remain missing.

In Febuary 2018, Boko Haram kidnapped another 110 school girls in Northeastern Nigeria. ​

Evidence shows that violence against women in IDP camps persist, Security officials often rape and abuse women in IDP camps in exchange for freedom to move around camps.


Violence Toward Children

55% of individuals affected by the conflict in Northeast Nigeria are children.

Approximately 244,000 children are currently severely malnourished, with about 49,000 at risk of death, according to the ​World Food Programme.

10 IDPs have died​ every day since the start of the insurgency, with a majority of them being children.
